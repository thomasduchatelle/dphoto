Design Direction: Key Decisions

Background: Dark blue gradient (#12242e core) instead of pure black. Creates depth and complements brand blue #185986. Gradient adds subtle visual interest without distraction.

No Full Borders: Album cards use background color differentiation instead of borders. Cleaner, more sophisticated look. Card background (#0d1d28 to #162936) contrasts with page gradient naturally.

Photo Grid: 2√ó2 layout (instead of 1√ó4) creates more balanced, square-ish album cards. Better for responsive design and visual scanning. Multiple albums visible per row on desktop.

Typography: Georgia serif for album names (luxury feel from Option C). Courier New monospace for dates (technical feel from Option A). Creates nice contrast between elegant content and technical metadata.

Text Overlay: Two variants shown. First has text below photos with gradient overlay. Second has text fully over photos with stronger darkening. Both darken images slightly to ensure readability with white text.

Hover States: Subtle translateY(-6px) lift on hover. No border color changes needed. Alternative variant adds blue gradient overlay on hover for extra feedback.

Spacing: 32px gap between album cards. Generous but not excessive. Cards feel distinct but cohesive. Random highlights use 16px gap for tighter grouping.

Brand Blue Usage: Section title underlines, photo count text, hover effects, navigation UI. Used sparingly for accent, not overwhelming. Complements the dark blue background beautifully.
